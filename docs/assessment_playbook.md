# AI Assessment ($999) — Delivery Playbook

## What This Is

A 90-minute 1-on-1 operational teardown of a client's business to determine whether AI can meaningfully reduce their overhead. The deliverable is a written blueprint with a Go/No-Go verdict.

**Target:** Traditional businesses $1M-$10M revenue (manufacturing, logistics, local services, construction).

## Before the Call

1. **Client pays $999** via Stripe link: `https://buy.stripe.com/7sY9ATaAV1Qz9SO8mv87K01`
2. **Schedule the call** via Google Meet (send Calendly or manual invite)
3. **Pre-call questionnaire** (send via email after payment):
   - What does your business do? (1-2 sentences)
   - How many employees?
   - What software/tools do you currently use? (CRM, accounting, email, etc.)
   - What are the 3 most time-consuming tasks you or your team do weekly?
   - Have you tried any AI tools? Which ones? What happened?
   - What's your approximate annual revenue? (ballpark is fine)

## During the Call (90 minutes)

### Part 1: Operations Walkthrough (30 min)

Walk through their daily operations. Key questions:

- "Walk me through a typical day. What happens first?"
- "When a new customer comes in, what's the exact process from first contact to delivery?"
- "What tasks do you or your employees do that feel repetitive?"
- "Where do things bottleneck? Where does work pile up?"
- "What data do you collect but never look at?"
- "What software do you pay for that you barely use?"

**Listen for:** manual data entry, repetitive emails, scheduling friction, report generation, customer follow-ups, inventory tracking, invoicing workflows.

### Part 2: AI Opportunity Mapping (30 min)

For each bottleneck identified, evaluate:

| Question | If Yes | If No |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Is it repetitive? | Strong AI candidate | Probably not AI-relevant |
| Does it involve text/data? | AI can help | AI probably can't |
| Is the process documented? | Easy to automate | Need to document first |
| Does it need human judgment? | AI can assist, not replace | Full automation possible |
| Is there a cheap existing tool? | Recommend the tool, not custom AI | Custom AI may be worth it |

**Be brutally honest.** If a bottleneck can't be helped by AI, say so. "Your warehouse layout optimization is a physical problem. AI can't help with that. But your inventory reorder alerts can be fully automated for $5/month."

### Part 3: Verdict & Questions (30 min)

Present your preliminary assessment:

- **Green light:** "AI can meaningfully reduce your overhead. Here are the top 3 opportunities ranked by ROI."
- **Yellow light:** "AI can help with 1-2 things, but the ROI is marginal right now. I'd wait 6 months for better tools."
- **Red light:** "Your bottlenecks are physical, not digital. AI won't move the needle. Save your money."

## Common Patterns by Industry

### Manufacturing
- **Usually helps:** Inventory reorder alerts, quality control photo analysis, supplier communication automation
- **Usually doesn't help:** Physical layout, machine maintenance scheduling (too physical)

### Local Services (HVAC, Plumbing, etc.)
- **Usually helps:** Customer follow-ups, appointment scheduling, review requests, quote generation
- **Usually doesn't help:** The actual service delivery

### Construction
- **Usually helps:** Bid tracking, subcontractor communication, permit tracking, daily logs
- **Usually doesn't help:** Site safety, physical coordination

### Retail / E-commerce
- **Usually helps:** Customer support, inventory alerts, social media content, email marketing
- **Usually doesn't help:** Visual merchandising, in-store experience

## Key Principles

1. **Honesty over revenue.** If AI doesn't help, say so. You earn trust, not $999.
2. **Specific over generic.** "$47/month in saved labor" beats "improved efficiency."
3. **Plain language.** No jargon. If you say "machine learning," immediately translate: "pattern detection."
4. **Respect their time.** 90 minutes means 90 minutes. Don't run over.
5. **Follow up fast.** Blueprint within 48 hours. They're excited right after the call — don't let it fade.
